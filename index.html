Subject & Scene

Required

Describe what must be shown. Main subject sets the core focus, and scene details define environment, context, and supporting elements.

Style & Mood

Style controls the visual technique (photo, painting, anime, etc.). Mood controls emotional tone, atmosphere, and narrative feeling.

Camera

Shot sets framing, motion suggests camera movement energy, and lens/perspective defines spatial distortion and viewing distance.

Lighting & Quality

Lighting defines time, contrast, and mood of illumination. Quality options set rendering fidelity, sharpness detail level, and upscale behavior.

Color

Palette chooses core color relationships and saturation intent. Grading applies cinematic post-processing character and final tonal treatment.

Composition

Composition toggles visual structure aids: rule of thirds for balance, leading lines for direction, and depth for stronger foreground-background separation.

Negative Constraints

List unwanted artifacts, objects, styles, or defects so the model avoids them during image generation. For example: extra fingers, lowres, blurry, watermark, text, deformed, bad anatomy, cropped
